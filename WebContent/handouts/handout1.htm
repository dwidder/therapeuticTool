WHAT IS A CONCUSSION?                

 

 

·      Concussion is considered to be a type of BRAIN INJURY

 

·      It results from BIOMECHANICAL FORCES

 

·      Effects are almost always TEMPORARY but may PERSIST in some people for an extended time

 

·      Loss of consciousness does NOT usually accompany concussion

 

·      Signs and symptoms of a concussion are often DIFFICULT TO IDENTIFY

 

·      For this reason, it is important to SEEK TREATMENT from a medical professional (McCrory et al, 2013)

 

 

 

 

 

COMMON CONCUSSION SYMPTOMS:

The signs and symptoms of a concussion can be SUBTLE AND MAY NOT BE IMMEDIATELY APPARENT. Symptoms can last for DAYS, WEEKS OR EVEN LONGER.

 

There are four categories of concussion symptoms (cdc.gov, 2014)

WHAT YOU CAN’T SEE ON BRAIN SCANS:

·      There are CHEMICAL CHANGES in the brain after concussion

·      Researchers have identified what they call a NEUROMETABOLIC CASCADE

·      The human brain has approximately 100 billion NEURONS, cells that process and transmit information through electrical and chemical signals

·      Immediately after the injury, there is a massive RELEASE OF NEUROTRANSITTERS, the brain chemicals that send signals from your brain to body

·      Your body has to work hard to RESTORE THE CHEMICALS BACK to the normal state

·      While the brain is recovering, your blood flow in the brain may be REDUCED to half the capacity

·      CHANGES TO MEMORY OR BEHAVIOR may occur because your brain is not getting the supply of oxygen it needs to function properly

·      A concussion occasionally leads to bleeding in or around the brain causing symptoms such as CONFUSION OR DROWSINESS RIGHT AWAY OR SOMETIMES LATER It is important to SEE A MEDICAL PROFESSIONAL if you have these symptoms

(Giza & Hovda, 2001; Parker, 2012).
